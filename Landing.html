Chandrayaan -3

Chandrayaan is the name of India's lunar exploration program.

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar mission, launched in 2008, which made significant discoveries about the Moon, including the presence of water molecules on the lunar surface. Chandrayaan-2, launched in 2019, was a more ambitious mission that included an orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan).

Unfortunately, the Vikram lander's attempt to soft-land on the lunar surface did not succeed as planned. Chandrayaan-3, if it were to be developed, would likely be a follow-up mission aimed at achieving a successful soft landing on the Moon's surface, building upon the lessons learned from Chandrayaan-2.
